Student FAQs

Real Questions Students Ask Before Choosing a Path

Practical, reassuring answers focused on career direction and next steps.

I'm confused between multiple career options. What should I do?

Start by mapping your strengths, interests, and time commitment. A counselor helps you compare options side-by-side and choose the most practical fit.

Can I enter tech without a coding background?

Yes. Many learners start from non-technical backgrounds. The right path depends on your goals, learning pace, and willingness to build foundational skills.

What if I choose the wrong course?

That is exactly why counseling matters first. We reduce this risk by validating role fit, required skills, and realistic outcomes before you commit.

How do I know which field fits me best?

We evaluate your profile using career intent, aptitude signals, and role expectations, then shortlist practical options instead of giving generic advice.

Can I switch careers even without experience?

Yes, with a structured transition plan. We help you leverage transferable skills and build the minimum portfolio needed to become interview-ready.

How long does it realistically take to become job-ready?

For many learners, focused consistency over a few months creates strong momentum. Exact timelines vary by background, target role, and daily effort.

Do I need a degree to start a tech career?

Not always. In many entry-level roles, demonstrable skills, projects, and interview readiness can matter more than formal degree specialization.

Will I get guidance after counseling?

Yes. Counseling is not a one-time conversation. You can continue with mentorship checkpoints and roadmap reviews as you progress.

What if I'm not confident in my skills yet?

Low confidence is common in early stages. We break your roadmap into clear milestones so progress feels measurable and achievable.
